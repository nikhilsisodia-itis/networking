# TCP-IP Transport Layer

## Introduction to the Transport Layer

- The Transport layer is responsible for delivering data between devices on a network.
- It ensures that data is sent and received accurately and in the correct order.
- The Transport layer manages end-to-end communication and error checking.
- It breaks down large data into smaller packets for easier transmission and reassembles them at the destination.

## Protocols used by the Transport Layer

- **TCP (Transmission Control Protocol):**
  - Creates a reliable connection between sender and receiver, before sending any data.
  - Break the data into smaller packets.
  - Ensures reliable delivery of data.
  - Checks for errors and makes sure data arrives in the correct order.
  - Resends lost or damaged data.

- **UDP (User Datagram Protocol):**
  - Do not create a connection before sending data.
  - Sends data quickly without establishing a connection.
  - Sends data quickly without checking for errors.
  - Does not guarantee that data arrives in order or even at all.
  - Useful for applications where speed is more important than reliability, like video streaming or online gaming.
